# Tennessee AI Relationship-Wellness Positioning

This document supersedes state-specific Illinois commentary for the current Tennessee-based product launch.

## Current Tennessee rule

Tennessee Public Chapter 647, effective July 1, 2026, prohibits a person who develops or deploys an artificial-intelligence system from advertising or representing that the system is, or is able to act as, a qualified mental-health professional. A violation is treated as an unfair or deceptive act under the Tennessee Consumer Protection Act.

US, FOR REAL must therefore be presented as:

- relationship-wellness software;
- guided self-help and structured reflection;
- communication and conflict-navigation support;
- an AI-assisted educational tool;
- a way to prepare for, supplement, or locate human support.

It must not be presented as:

- a therapist or psychotherapist;
- a qualified mental-health professional;
- diagnosis, treatment, or clinical care;
- an emergency or crisis-response service;
- a substitute for a licensed professional.

## Required product behavior

1. Display a clear AI identity and limitation disclosure in account, safety, and serious-session surfaces.
2. Avoid names, marketing language, onboarding language, and AI responses that imply professional credentials or human identity.
3. Keep application code in control of privacy scope, safety branches, workflow stages, output schemas, evidence provenance, and crisis handoff.
4. Stop ordinary relationship exercises when fear, coercion, stalking, threats, physical violence, sexual violence, self-harm risk, or harm-to-others risk is disclosed.
5. Do not advise confrontation in unsafe situations.
6. Give users an immediate route to trained human support.
7. Never fabricate agreement, diagnose a person, or present an absent partner's presumed thoughts as facts.
8. Keep owner-only relationship disclosures out of advertising and third-party targeting systems.

## Tennessee human-support routing

- Immediate danger: call 911.
- Mental-health or emotional crisis: call or text 988 and press 0 for a Tennessee crisis counselor.
- Tennessee Behavioral Health Helpline: 800-560-5767.
- Tennessee REDLINE for substance-use treatment referral: 800-889-9789.
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 800-799-7233 or text START to 88788.
- Tennessee Behavioral Health Safety Net: eligibility-based outpatient services for uninsured adults across all 95 counties.

## Claims policy

Do not claim guaranteed relationship improvement, guaranteed reconciliation, guaranteed safety, clinical effectiveness, or equivalence to therapy. The product may make verifiable process commitments, including:

- equal solo and linked access;
- owner-only private-session storage;
- no absent-partner mind-reading;
- no fabricated mutual agreement;
- visible evidence and hypothesis labels;
- safety interruption and human-resource routing;
- correction, export, and deletion controls;
- measurable behavior-experiment follow-up.

Outcome claims should be based on prospective evaluation, documented adverse-event monitoring, and independent clinical and legal review.
